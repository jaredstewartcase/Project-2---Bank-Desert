Charting Financial Exclusion in Los Angeles


Proposal

In consideration of the documented research on the unbanked and underbanked, we will explore the geographic availability of federally-insured banks versus alternative financial services in Los Angeles neighborhoods. We will compare the availability of these services with median income, racial diversity (as measured by the diversity index), and population density. We aim to prove that a lack of traditional banking institutions and a high density of alternative financial systems will correlate with low-income communities and communities of color.


Why Los Angeles?

Los Angeles is one of the most racially diverse cities in the United States. It is also one of the most economically stratified cities.The range in income and diversity will give us an accurate portrayal of bank deserts in major metropolitan American cities.
